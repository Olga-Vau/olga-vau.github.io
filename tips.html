Recommendations for Practices and Devices That Can Contribute to Optimal Oral Health:

  • Toothbrush: Use a soft-bristled toothbrush that is comfortable to hold and allows you to reach all areas of your mouth. Replace your toothbrush every three to four months or sooner if the bristles become frayed.
  • Electric Toothbrush: Consider using an electric toothbrush, which can provide more effective plaque removal compared to manual brushing. Look for an electric toothbrush with a rotating or oscillating head and features such as a built-in timer and pressure sensor.
  • Fluoride Toothpaste: Choose a fluoride toothpaste approved by the ADA. Fluoride helps strengthen tooth enamel and protect against tooth decay. Apply a pea-sized amount of toothpaste to your toothbrush.
  • Dental Floss: Use dental floss or interdental cleaners to clean between your teeth and along the gumline. Flossing helps remove plaque and debris that brushing alone may miss. Choose floss that works best for your teeth and technique, such as traditional floss, floss picks, or water flossers.
  • Mouthwash: Rinse with an antimicrobial mouthwash after brushing and flossing to help kill bacteria and freshen your breath. Look for a mouthwash that carries the ADA seal of approval and addresses your specific oral health needs, such as anti-plaque or anti-gingivitis properties.
  • Tongue Cleaner: Use a tongue cleaner or a toothbrush with a tongue-cleaning feature to gently remove bacteria and debris from your tongue. This can help improve oral hygiene and prevent bad breath.
  • Water Flosser: Consider using a water flosser (oral irrigator) as an alternative to traditional flossing. Water flossers use a stream of water to clean between teeth and along the gumline. They can be especially beneficial for individuals with orthodontic appliances or difficulty using traditional floss.
  • Interdental Brushes: Interdental brushes (proxy brushes) are small, thin brushes designed to clean between teeth and around dental work like braces or bridges. They can be an effective tool for removing plaque and debris from hard-to-reach areas.
  • Dental Picks or Stimulators: Dental picks or stimulators can be used to clean between teeth or massage the gums. They can help dislodge food particles and stimulate blood circulation in the gums.
  • Nightguards or Mouthguards: If you grind your teeth (bruxism) or participate in contact sports, consider using a nightguard or mouthguard. These devices help protect your teeth from damage and prevent injuries.
  • Dental Sealants: Dental sealants are thin, protective coatings applied to the chewing surfaces of the back teeth (molars) to prevent tooth decay. They are especially beneficial for children and teenagers but can be recommended for adults as well.

    Remember to consult with your dentist or dental hygienist for personalized recommendations regarding specific devices and practices that would be most beneficial for your oral health needs.
